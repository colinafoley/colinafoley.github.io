In this day and age of hyper-connectivity, digital socialization, and information security breaches such as MySpace, LinkedIn, and iMesh, it is difficult to maintain your online identity while cultivating a digital presence that emphasizes who you really are and who you want to be online. In this session we'll explore through discussion, and maybe even some role-playing, concepts of digital identity creation, maintenance, and protection.

Perspectives

a set of credentials

data fields

juicy content

a pivot point further into an organization, network, or group of people

We're going to look at multiple of these perspectives

credentials: a username, password, and possibly a corresponding email address

In many cases, you ARE your logon. This is why it's called authentication. Your credentials verify the authenticity of the actor involved.

Giving away your credentials, is like giving away your identity

data fields, as in data breach

juicy content: maybe you have some sensitive photos or research data on your hands

pivot point: maybe you're just the gateway to something bigger, like a faculty member or your parents bank account
